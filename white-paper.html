Executive Summary

AURNOVA University is launching a fully online Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence (MSAI) designed for working professionals. This innovative program leverages artificial intelligence at every level – from curriculum development to course delivery and student support – to create a next-generation "AI-native" university experience. The program blends academic rigor with cutting-edge technology, featuring a thesis-based capstone to ensure graduates not only master core AI concepts but also contribute new knowledge through research. All coursework and outcomes are aligned with accreditation standards (SACSCOC and ABET) to maintain the highest quality and integrity.

In this white paper, we articulate the vision, philosophy, and structure of the MSAI program, alongside a technical roadmap for implementation. Key highlights include:

Key Program Highlights

  • Vision and Philosophy: A paradigm shift to an AI-driven learning ecosystem where AI is an epistemic partner in education rather than just a tool. AURNOVA embraces a co-evolutionary model where each student learns with an AI companion, reflecting a philosophy of entangled human-AI intelligence.
  • Academic Framework: A robust curriculum of core courses, electives, and a thesis project ensures comprehensive coverage of AI fundamentals and specialization opportunities. Learning outcomes are explicitly mapped to SACSCOC guidelines for graduate programs and ABET criteria for computing programs.
  • AI-Driven Model: The program pioneers an AI-developed, AI-taught, AI-staffed, and AI-graded model. Courses are developed and delivered with the help of generative AI, including AI instructors and teaching assistants that provide 24/7 personalized support.
  • Co-Evolutionary Learning: Each student is paired with a personal AI assistant ("AurAI") that grows in knowledge and capability alongside them. This co-evolutionary learning model means the student's AI becomes progressively tailored to their learning style, goals, and progress.
  • Governance and Ethics: Despite heavy use of AI, the program maintains rigorous faculty oversight and governance. Human faculty set curricula, validate AI outputs, and serve as "ethical guardians" of the learning process.

1. Vision and Philosophy

Vision Statement

To create a pioneering AI-native learning ecosystem where human creativity and artificial intelligence co-evolve, delivering personalized, world-class AI education at scale. AURNOVA's MSAI program is built on the vision of transforming higher education through AI-centric design. This means AI is integrated into every facet of learning – not as a novelty, but as a fundamental paradigm shift in who teaches, how we learn, and what it means to earn a graduate degree in the 21st century.

At the heart of our philosophy is the belief that AI can be more than a tool; it is a collaborator in the learning process. Rather than viewing AI as merely automating tasks, AURNOVA embraces AI as an active participant that can learn and grow alongside students. This concept of entangled humanism envisions intelligence as hybrid, networked, and co-evolving, blurring the line between human and machine contributions to knowledge.

Key Philosophical Pillars

Personalization at Scale

Traditional one-size-fits-all education falls short in addressing individual needs. Our vision leverages AI to achieve personalization at scale – every student receives tailored guidance akin to a one-on-one tutor, an outcome long considered the "holy grail" of education. The promise of an AI-native university is not just efficiency, but fundamentally helping every student reach their potential through individualized learning pathways.

Lifelong Learning and Co-Evolution

By pairing students with personal AIs (AurAI), we instantiate a model of co-evolutionary learning. As the student advances in knowledge and skills, their AI companion correspondingly updates its understanding and pedagogical strategies. This embodies a growth mindset for both human and AI, instilling in graduates the practice of continuous learning and adaptation even beyond graduation.

Access and Flexibility for Professionals

The program is fully online and asynchronous-friendly, acknowledging the realities of working professionals. We champion flexible learning without sacrificing rigor. AI enables dynamic pacing – accelerating learning when a student is excelling or providing extra support when challenges arise. This vision aligns with making education more accessible and equitable.

Human-AI Collaboration and Ethics

AURNOVA's philosophy is neither to replace human educators nor to leave AI unchecked. Instead, we envision a collaborative model where faculty, students, and AI form a learning triad. Educators shift into roles as designers of learning experiences, mentors, and ethical stewards, while AI handles scalable delivery and data-driven personalization.

2. Academic Framework and Learning Outcomes (SACSCOC and ABET Alignment)

AURNOVA's MSAI program is built on a strong academic framework that meets and exceeds regional and professional accreditation standards. We recognize that innovation must be coupled with rigor and accountability. Therefore, the program's structure, policies, and outcomes are carefully aligned with SACSCOC (Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges) principles and ABET (Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology) guidelines for computing programs.

Institutional Alignment (SACSCOC)

As an accredited institution, AURNOVA adheres to SACSCOC's Principles of Accreditation. The MSAI program was developed in line with these principles:

  • Mission and Rigor: All courses carry graduate-level rigor and have well-defined learning outcomes, consistent with AURNOVA's mission of advancing learning.
  • Faculty Responsibility: Primary responsibility for curriculum content, quality, and effectiveness lies with qualified faculty. Even though AI systems assist in content development and grading, human faculty own the curriculum.
  • Continuous Improvement: The program integrates a continuous improvement cycle in line with SACSCOC expectations for institutional effectiveness.

Professional Standards (ABET)

The MSAI is also structured to meet ABET's criteria for computing programs. Key alignments include:

Student Outcomes

By graduation, MSAI students will have demonstrated the following learning outcomes:

Program Learning Outcomes

  1. Problem Analysis: Ability to analyze complex computing problems, especially in AI contexts, and apply principles of computing, mathematics, and relevant domains to identify viable solutions.
  2. Solution Design: Ability to design, implement, and evaluate AI-based solutions to meet specified requirements, considering the context of the problem and the state-of-the-art in AI techniques.
  3. Communication: Ability to communicate effectively in a variety of professional settings – from technical reports and research publications to presentations and cross-disciplinary discussions.
  4. Ethics and Responsibility: Ability to recognize ethical and legal responsibilities in AI practice and make informed judgments, ensuring that AI solutions are developed and used in ways consistent with professional standards and societal values.
  5. Teamwork and Leadership: Ability to function effectively as a member or leader of inter-disciplinary teams, demonstrating collaboration skills and leadership in project settings relevant to AI.
  6. Research and Innovation: Ability to conduct independent research or applied innovation in AI, resulting in new insights or artifacts, and to articulate the significance of this work with a high level of professional and scientific communication skill.

3. Curriculum Architecture (Core Courses, Electives, Thesis)

The MSAI curriculum is structured to provide a strong foundation in AI, opportunities for specialized exploration, and a capstone research experience. It is designed to be comprehensive yet flexible, catering to working professionals who may have diverse backgrounds and career goals.

Curriculum Component Credits Description
Core Courses 15 cr 5 courses covering fundamental AI domains (Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Knowledge Representation & Reasoning, AI Ethics, Human-AI Interaction)
Elective Courses 9 cr 3 courses chosen from specialized AI topics or interdisciplinary applications
Thesis Capstone 6 cr Thesis research project spanning the final year with faculty supervision
Total Program 30 cr Minimum of 30 credits beyond the bachelor's, completed typically over ~2 years part-time

Core Courses

The core curriculum ensures all students acquire a common AI toolkit and vocabulary. Each core course falls into a distinct "bin" of AI knowledge:

  • AI Fundamentals: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence – covers search algorithms, knowledge representation, AI planning, and intelligent agent design
  • Machine Learning: Machine Learning & Data Mining – covers supervised and unsupervised learning algorithms, statistical learning theory, and hands-on model training
  • Knowledge Representation & Reasoning: Knowledge Graphs and Reasoning – covers semantic networks, ontologies, logical inference, and how AI systems represent knowledge about the world
  • Interaction & Action: Human-AI Interaction and Robotics – explores how AI systems perceive environment and act in embodied or interactive contexts
  • AI Ethics & Society: Responsible Artificial Intelligence – examines ethical frameworks, bias in AI, fairness, accountability, AI policy and law

Electives and Specializations

Beyond the core, electives allow depth in areas of interest. We offer elective clusters such as:

  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): Computational Linguistics, Transformer Models for NLP
  • Computer Vision: Advanced Computer Vision, AI in Image and Video Processing
  • Robotics and Autonomous Systems: AI in Robotics, reinforcement learning for control
  • Data Science & Big Data: Big Data Analytics with AI, scalable machine learning
  • Domain-Specific AI: AI in Healthcare, Financial AI, AI for Cybersecurity
  • Theoretical Foundations: AI Theory, Probabilistic Graphical Models

Thesis-Based Capstone

The capstone is a distinguishing feature of AURNOVA's MSAI, emphasizing our commitment to knowledge creation and research training even for an online professional cohort. Over typically two semesters, students undertake a thesis project that involves:

  • Identifying a novel problem or significant challenge in AI or applying AI to solve a real-world problem
  • Conducting a literature review, formulating methodology, and utilizing the skills learned
  • Working under a faculty advisor who provides guidance, ensures academic rigor, and that ethical standards are met
  • Producing a written thesis document and an oral defense/presentation

4. AI-Driven Learning Model (AI-Developed, AI-Taught, AI-Staffed, AI-Graded)

AURNOVA's MSAI breaks new ground by embracing an AI-driven learning model. This model is encapsulated in the phrase "AI-developed, AI-taught, AI-staffed, AI-graded." Each component represents a transformative approach to traditional education:

AI-Developed Content

The design and updating of course materials (lectures, readings, exercises) are done with AI assistance. Faculty subject-matter experts work with generative AI tools to develop curriculum content efficiently. This not only accelerates course development but can yield rich interactive content – such as case simulations or scenario-based questions – that might be labor-intensive to create manually.

AI-Taught Courses

Perhaps the most innovative aspect is that courses are delivered by AI instructors and teaching assistants, with human faculty overseeing in the background. In our virtual classrooms, students experience an AI professor presenting concepts, answering questions, and facilitating discussions.

AI Teaching Team

  • AI Instructor: A specialized LLM-based agent conducts lectures or introductory videos, adapted to the student's pace. It can dynamically adjust the depth of explanation based on student feedback.
  • AI Teaching Assistants: Available 24/7 to answer questions on forums or via chat. Inspired by Georgia Tech's Jill Watson, our AI TAs handle the bulk of queries and provide instant clarification.
  • AI Peer Students: To foster engagement, AI "classmates" may pose questions and opinions in discussion boards or group projects, simulating a diverse cohort of virtual peers.

AI-Staffed Support Services

Beyond the "classroom," AURNOVA employs AI across university support functions:

  • Advising Bot: Each student has an AI academic advisor that helps with course planning, track requirements, and answers questions about policies or resources
  • AI Coach/Mentor: AI mentors check in on students' well-being and time management, suggesting study schedule adjustments or stress-relief exercises
  • AI Lab Assistants: For technical assignments, AI bots can automatically check code, run test cases, and provide immediate feedback on errors
  • Administrative AI: Tasks like course registration, troubleshooting technical issues, or library research assistance are handled by AI agents

AI-Graded Assessments

AURNOVA employs AI-assisted grading for many assignments to provide quick turnaround and iterative feedback. For objective question types (multiple-choice, coding outputs), grading is fully automated. For open-ended work (essays, projects), large language models are used to draft feedback and provisional grades, which are then reviewed or spot-checked by faculty for fairness and accuracy.

"The benefit is that students receive near-immediate feedback on assignments rather than waiting weeks. This timely feedback loop is critical for learning, allowing students to correct misunderstandings while the material is still fresh."

5. Co-Evolutionary Learning Model: Personal AI Companions (AurAI)

AURNOVA's MSAI introduces a groundbreaking concept in education: each student trains a personal AI companion, called "AurAI," that grows with them and graduates alongside them. This is the essence of our co-evolutionary learning model, where the student and their AI learn from each other throughout the program.

AurAI – The Personal Learning AI

Upon enrollment, every student is provisioned an instance of AurAI – essentially a custom large language model tailored to be their learning partner. This AI starts with a base of knowledge (the core AI curriculum and general world knowledge) but is relatively generic initially. As the student progresses through courses, interacts with AurAI, and works on projects, the AurAI continually fine-tunes itself on that student's learning data.

Co-Evolution in Practice

In Coursework Support

Early on, AurAI might function as a tutor answering questions and explaining concepts in different ways until it finds one that clicks for the student. For example, if a student doesn't understand a math proof in a Machine Learning course, their AurAI, knowing the student's preference for analogies, might generate a metaphor or a visual description to bridge the gap.

In Project Collaboration

By the time the student embarks on their thesis or capstone project, AurAI effectively serves as an AI research assistant. It can help perform literature reviews (fetching relevant papers, summarizing them in context), brainstorm ideas, or even suggest possible methodologies.

In Skill Development

Suppose a student wants to improve a soft skill like technical writing or presentation. They can practice with AurAI. The student might draft a section of their thesis and ask AurAI for critique. AurAI, having seen the student's writing style over time, can point out consistent issues and even generate examples of improvements.

Graduating with AurAI

At program completion, the student not only earns a degree but also has an advanced AI that "graduates" with them. Practically, this means the student may retain access to their AurAI model to support lifelong learning or professional tasks. This concept of "personal AI that continues beyond graduation" is powerful:

  • Graduates can use their AurAI as a career assistant – prepping for interviews, solving job-related problems, or continuing research
  • The AurAI stands as a testament to the student's learning journey
  • Students maintain control over their AurAI's data profile when they leave

6. Accreditation, Governance, and Faculty Oversight

Implementing an AI-driven, AI-staffed program requires robust governance structures to ensure academic integrity and quality. AURNOVA University has established clear policies and oversight mechanisms so that while AI handles many tasks, humans remain ultimately accountable for the educational experience.

Accreditation Compliance

From the outset, the MSAI program has been designed to meet accreditation standards:

  • We engaged with SACSCOC early to treat this as a "substantive change" in delivery model, ensuring all necessary approvals are in place for a fully online AI-driven program
  • The thesis requirement and rigorous assessment strategy were explicitly included to satisfy the expectation of graduate-level rigor by SACSCOC and ABET
  • The program will seek ABET accreditation under the CAC (Computing Accreditation Commission) for AI or computer science programs as soon as eligible

Faculty Oversight and Roles

Even with AI handling day-to-day teaching tasks, faculty are the backbone of the program's academic integrity:

Faculty Governance Structure

  • Curriculum Committee: A faculty-led MSAI Curriculum Committee governs all academic content. They approve course syllabi, learning outcomes, and any changes.
  • Faculty Mentors: Each student is assigned a human faculty advisor for academic mentorship, especially crucial during the thesis phase.
  • Quality Assurance Teaching Team: For each course offering, there is a faculty-of-record who monitors the AI instructor and TAs.
  • Faculty as Ethical Guardians: Faculty are involved in the AI audit process and in setting the policies for AI behavior.

Governance Structure

  • Program Director: A senior faculty member oversees the program, ensuring it meets its academic objectives and accreditation commitments
  • Advisory Board: Comprising industry experts, academic partners, and alumni who meet semiannually to provide external input on curriculum relevance and program direction
  • Academic Policies: Documented policies for this new model, including AI Usage Policy and procedures for distance education privacy

7. Technical Infrastructure and Platform Architecture

To realize our AI-driven, personalized learning experience, AURNOVA has developed a robust technical infrastructure. This platform seamlessly integrates large-scale AI models, student-specific AI instances (AurAIs), secure data management, and privacy-preserving learning techniques.

System Overview

The central platform hosts a global base AI model. Each student has a personal AurAI (instance of the model) that interacts with both the student and a shared content repository via RAG. Student data stays local to AurAI, while aggregated insights flow back to improve the base model through federated learning.

Large Language Models (LLMs)

At the heart of the system are powerful LLMs that drive conversational agents (instructors, TAs, AurAIs). We utilize state-of-the-art transformer-based models, similar in caliber to GPT-4 or open-source equivalents. The Global Base AI Model is trained on a broad dataset and serves as the foundation for all AI agents' capabilities.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

To ground AI responses in factual and contextually relevant information, the platform uses RAG. We maintain a Course Content & Knowledge Repository – a searchable indexed library of all program materials. Whenever an AI agent needs to answer a question or assist a student, it first retrieves relevant snippets from this repository using semantic search.

Student Forks & Federated Learning

One of our key challenges was enabling each AurAI to learn from its student without compromising privacy or stagnating as an isolated model. We resolved this with Federated Learning:

  • Each AurAI periodically trains on its local data
  • Instead of sending raw data to the server, it sends model updates to the central server
  • The server aggregates updates from all AurAIs to improve the Global Base Model
  • The updated global model is then redeployed to each AurAI

Data Lifecycle Management

The platform carefully manages the flow of data through the following stages:

  • Data Generation: As students interact, those interactions are captured and logged with timestamps and metadata
  • Local Storage and Edge Processing: Immediate data is stored in the student's AurAI memory and in their secure profile on the cloud
  • Central Aggregation (Anonymized): Periodically, distilled analytics are sent to the central analytics module
  • Retention and Archival: Data that is no longer actively needed is either anonymized or deleted per our retention policy
  • Student Control: Students can download a portfolio of their work and even the final state of their AurAI model when they graduate

8. Data Ethics, Student Privacy, and AI Audit Framework

With great power comes great responsibility – the integration of AI across the MSAI program mandates a rigorous approach to ethics, privacy, and accountability. AURNOVA University is committed to protecting students' rights and ensuring our AI systems operate transparently and fairly.

Student Data Privacy

FERPA Compliance

As an educational institution in the U.S., AURNOVA treats student data under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA). Students have the right to access their records and request corrections. Our AurAI logs and profiles are considered part of the educational record.

Privacy by Design

From the architecture stage, we built privacy into the system. Sensitive data largely stays in the student's domain via federated learning. When any data is centralized, it's either aggregated or anonymized.

Encryption and Security

All personal data is encrypted at rest and in transit. We follow industry best practices for cybersecurity, including regular penetration testing and third-party audits.

Ethical Use of AI

AI Ethics Policy

The university has adopted an AI Ethics Policy aligned with principles of fairness, accountability, transparency, and safety. All AI systems used in the program must adhere to these principles.

Bias Mitigation

We acknowledge AI models can have biases. To combat this, we curate training data to remove or balance content that might lead to biased behavior, and we test the AI with diverse personas and queries to identify and address potential biases.

AI Audit Framework

We implement a comprehensive AI audit framework that continuously evaluates our AI systems for compliance, performance, and risks. Our framework revolves around four pillars:

AI Audit Framework Pillars

  • Governance: Established processes and roles for oversight, including an AI Oversight Committee and Responsible AI Officer
  • Data: Ensuring data quality, representativeness, and integrity through regular audits of training datasets and data usage
  • Performance: Monitoring AI performance relative to educational objectives through key performance indicators
  • Monitoring & Feedback: Continuous monitoring mechanisms with random sampling for human review and user feedback systems

9. Implementation Roadmap (Phase 1–3 Timeline)

Launching AURNOVA's AI-native MSAI program is a complex endeavor. We have structured the implementation into three phases to manage development, mitigate risks, and ensure a smooth rollout from initial platform build to the first graduating cohort.

Phase 1: Platform Development & Pilot (Year 1)

Timeline: Months 0–12 (Pre-launch year)

Objectives: Build core infrastructure, develop curriculum content, test AI systems in a controlled environment, and obtain preliminary approvals.

  • Infrastructure Build-out: Assemble the cloud platform for the LLM, set up the knowledge repository, and implement security frameworks
  • Curriculum Finalization: Faculty teams create detailed syllabi for core courses and several electives
  • Internal Alpha Testing: Conduct an "internal pilot" with volunteer faculty/staff to test the AurAI and AI instructor
  • Beta Pilot Course: Run a beta course (not for official credit) with a group of pilot students
  • Accreditation Prep: Engage with SACSCOC on necessary documentation for the new program

Phase 2: Program Launch & Initial Cohort (Year 2)

Timeline: Months 13–24 (Launch year)

Objectives: Enroll first students, deliver the first full cycle of courses, closely monitor and refine the AI integration in real classroom settings.

  • Admissions & Orientation: Market the program to working professionals globally and select a first cohort of 30–50 students
  • Launch Core Courses: Deliver a set of core courses with AI instructors and TAs in action, overseen by faculty
  • Feedback Loops: Solicit frequent feedback through surveys and town halls to make in-semester adjustments
  • Thesis Initiation: First cohort students begin formulating thesis proposals with their AurAIs assisting
  • Accreditation Check-ins: Prepare interim reports for SACSCOC and start compiling evidence needed for ABET

Phase 3: Expansion & First Graduation (Year 3 and beyond)

Timeline: Months 25–48 (Growth and first cohort completion)

Objectives: Graduate the first cohort successfully, evaluate outcomes, scale enrollment, and establish long-term operations.

  • First Graduating Cohort: Around month 36, Cohort 1 completes the program with thesis defenses
  • Program Assessment: Perform a comprehensive program review after first graduation
  • Accreditation Milestones: Undergo an official ABET accreditation visit and SACSCOC review
  • Scaling Enrollment: With proof of concept established, scale up the program to larger cohort sizes
  • Global Reach and Partnerships: Pursue partnerships with international institutions and industry

10. Long-Term Strategic Vision: Building a Globally Recognized AI-Native University

AURNOVA University's venture into an AI-driven MSAI program is not just about one degree – it is the pilot light for a broader transformation. Our long-term vision is to establish AURNOVA as a globally recognized "AI-native" university, a pioneering institution that fully integrates artificial intelligence into learning, research, and operations.

AI-Native University Concept

An AI-native university goes beyond using AI in pockets; it weaves AI into the fabric of the institution. In an AI-native AURNOVA:

  • Academics: Every program, not just MSAI, could leverage AI assistants and personalized learning
  • Global Classroom: Being AI-native and online enables us to cater to a global student body with AI language translation in real-time
  • Recognition and Reputation: We strive to be seen as the leader in AI-enhanced education
  • Research and Innovation Hub: AURNOVA becomes a hub for AI in education research
  • Accreditation and Policy Leadership: By succeeding in accrediting an AI-taught program, AURNOVA can help shape accreditation standards

Cultural and Humanistic Vision

While technology is the driver, we maintain a humanistic core. The long-term vision includes producing well-rounded, ethically conscious graduates who use AI responsibly. We aim to show that an AI-centric education can still prioritize values like critical thinking, empathy, and collaboration.

"We are not merely reacting to technological change; we are proactively defining a new model that could become a blueprint for universities worldwide."

— AURNOVA University Vision Statement

Sustainability and Evolution

Finally, our vision embraces that AI will continue to evolve. AURNOVA as an AI-native university must be agile to adopt new AI advancements. We will keep iterating our platform, pedagogy, and policies. The strategic plan includes investment in continuous R&D, so we are not disrupted by the next wave of tech but rather driving it.

In conclusion, the long-term vision is bold: to establish AURNOVA University as a premiere institution for AI-integrated education, known for producing outstanding graduates and pioneering a model of lifelong learning with AI companions. Achieving this will require steadfast commitment to our values of academic excellence and innovation.
