Core Courses (Required)

These foundational courses provide the essential knowledge base for all MS AI students.

MSAI 501
Foundations of Artificial Intelligence
3 Credits
Core
Comprehensive introduction to the fundamental concepts, techniques, and applications of artificial intelligence. Covers search algorithms, knowledge representation, reasoning, and problem-solving methods.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the history and evolution of AI
  • Master search algorithms and heuristics
  • Learn knowledge representation methods
  • Apply logical reasoning techniques
Prerequisites

Undergraduate degree in Computer Science, Mathematics, or related field

Detailed Syllabus - MSAI 501

Week 1-2: Introduction to AI
  • History and evolution of artificial intelligence
  • AI problem-solving approaches
  • Intelligent agents and environments
Week 3-4: Search Algorithms
  • Uninformed search (BFS, DFS, UCS)
  • Informed search (A*, greedy search)
  • Local search and optimization
Week 5-6: Knowledge Representation
  • Propositional logic
  • First-order logic
  • Knowledge graphs and ontologies
Week 7-8: Reasoning and Planning
  • Logical inference
  • Automated planning
  • Constraint satisfaction problems
Week 9-10: Machine Learning Foundations
  • Supervised learning overview
  • Unsupervised learning concepts
  • Reinforcement learning basics
Week 11-12: AI Applications
  • Natural language processing
  • Computer vision applications
  • Expert systems
Week 13-14: AI Ethics and Future
  • Ethical considerations in AI
  • Bias and fairness
  • Future directions in AI research
Week 15: Final Project Presentations

MSAI 602
Master's Thesis
6 Credits
Thesis
Independent research project culminating in a written thesis and oral defense. Students work closely with faculty advisors on original AI research.

Detailed Syllabus - MSAI 602

Semester 1: Research Planning
  • Literature review completion
  • Research methodology design
  • Preliminary experiments
Semester 2: Implementation and Analysis
  • Data collection and processing
  • Algorithm implementation
  • Experimental validation
Semester 3: Writing and Defense
  • Thesis writing
  • Peer review and revision
  • Oral defense preparation
Deliverables
  • Written thesis (50-80 pages)
  • Oral defense presentation
  • Code repository and documentation
  • Research poster (optional)
Evaluation Criteria
  • Originality and contribution
  • Technical rigor and methodology
  • Writing quality and clarity
  • Defense performance
